Section I // Core Mechanic

The Operation Grid

A 4×3 matrix. Rows are phases of an operation in time. Columns are modes of force. You assign crew to cells before the round resolves. The GM holds the opposition grid in secret. You never see it. You read the world and bet.

Section II // Lines of Effort

The Three Columns

Columns are not job classes. They are modes of applying force — ways of bringing capability to bear on a problem. Each beats one, loses to one. Your bet on which column dominates determines the outcome.

Column 01
Ghost

Everything that works by not being seen. Electronic intrusion, social manipulation, surveillance, disguise. A strong Ghost line means the target never fully understood what hit them. Netrunners threading ICE from a van three blocks away. Faces walking into the lobby on false credentials.

BEATS: ANCHOR — you can't hold what you can't see coming
LOSES TO: STRIKE — overwhelming force flushes concealment
Column 02
Strike

Speed, violence, and the direct application of overwhelming capability. The Solo kicking in the door at exactly the right moment. Strike doesn't win by being subtle — it wins by being faster and harder than the opposition can respond to. Run Strike cold and you turn a noise complaint into a massacre.

BEATS: GHOST — overwhelming force flushes concealment
LOSES TO: ANCHOR — a prepared position bleeds an assault
Column 03
Anchor

Presence, control, and sustained hold. The Tech hardwiring a backdoor that'll work three rounds later. The Medtech keeping the crew operational under suppression. The standing arrangement with the local Valentinos that keeps the block quiet tonight. Anchor compounds. Maelstrom knows this. Arasaka knows it better.

BEATS: STRIKE — a prepared defence bleeds an assault
LOSES TO: GHOST — you can't hold what you can't see coming
Section III // Temporal Architecture

The Four Phases

Every operation resolves across four phases in sequence. A failure in Phase 1 doesn't abort Phase 2 — it degrades it. Phase cascade means each outcome modifies the next. A botched Approach is survivable. A botched Breach while carrying a botched Approach is how crews get flatlined.

Phase 01
Approach

Everything before the point of no return. Surveillance, cover identities, bribery of perimeter assets, Netrunner prep. Ghost-dominant — arriving invisible means arriving ready. A Strike-heavy Approach signals an overt operation. Your opposition is already adjusting.

Ghost-dominant Recon Insertion
Phase 02
Breach

The moment the operation becomes irreversible. Physical entry, ICE penetration, access acquisition. This is the phase most sensitive to PvP collision — if a rival crew is running the same target, Phase 2 is where they collide. Neither player knew.

High risk PvP collision ICE
Phase 03
Execute

The primary objective. The data pull. The extraction. The deletion. The thing you were paid to do. Execute cashes in all previous phase advantages. A crew that bleeds through Breach arrives here wounded, loud, and running out of time. The opposition is almost always Anchor-dominant here.

Primary objective Anchor defence
Phase 04
Extract

More dangerous than most crews plan for. Night City's factions don't always respond during Breach — sometimes they wait. Road blocks, pursuit drones, burned safehouses. The crews that survive long-term plan Extract with the same rigour as Breach.

Escape Decoy Often underplanned
Section IV // Action Economy

Two Slots. Eight Options.

Each round you have exactly two Action Slots. Your crew members are your slots — you assign people, not abstract points. A crew member assigned to an action is unavailable for everything else that round. Two slots means every round you're choosing what not to do just as much as what to do.

2
Action_01
Engage

Run an operation. Build a grid. Commit crew to phases. The GM resolves against the target. Earn Eddies and Street Cred based on outcome rating. Crew gone 1–3 rounds.

Collision risk Eddies Street Cred
Action_02
Protocol

Transmit an encrypted message to another player through Night City's network. ICE strength determines what an interceptor can read. Nothing in Night City is secure — it's only unread.

Intercept risk Intel
Action_03
Transfer

Send crew members to another player's roster. Declare terms: permanent gift, loan, or conditional return. Whether they know they're being transferred — or think they're on an independent assignment — changes everything.

Betrayal vector
Action_04
Ghost

Go completely dark. Costs both action slots. No jobs, no comms, no visible activity. Heat drops –1 automatically. You cannot be targeted by Leverage or Surveil. Intercept attempts against you auto-fail.

Heat –1 Untargetable
Action_05
Acquire

Recruit a new crew member or upgrade an existing one. All new recruits carry a hidden loyalty rating the GM assigns. You learn it through gameplay — how they perform under pressure, what they do when offered alternatives.

Hidden loyalty Eddies cost
Action_06
Leverage

Apply pressure to a faction or player. No grid required. Demand withdrawal, force intelligence disclosure, call in a contract. Requires FA or SO rated 3+. Failed leverage hardens the target and reveals your intent.

Blowback risk
Action_07
Surveil

Watch a target or player without committing an operation. Intel feeds next round's decisions. Requires NR or CT. If another player is surveilling you while you surveil them — you both know someone is watching. You don't know who.

Intel gain Counter-surveillance

Dedicate your Netrunner entirely to defence. Doubles your Protocol ICE for the round. Degrades incoming Surveil intel. Reduces Leverage effectiveness. Your NR cannot run any Engage operation this round.

Section V // Comms & Intercept

Protocol — Talking in Night City

Protocol is not a free email. You're transmitting an encrypted data packet through Night City's infrastructure. That infrastructure has owners. Those owners have interests. And other players have Netrunners.

ICE Rating Table

ICE Cost Protection Level
ICE-1 Free Minimal. Any NR-2+ reads the full message.
ICE-2 Assign NR-2+ Standard. Requires NR-3+ to breach. NR-2 sees metadata only.
ICE-3 Assign NR-4+ Strong. NR-3 sees that a message was sent, not its content.
ICE-4 Assign NR-5 Military-grade. Only NR-5 can breach. Others see noise.
ICE-5 NR-5 + ED-3k Black ICE. Breach attempt wounds the intercepting Netrunner.

Intercept Results

Result What the Interceptor Receives
NR beats ICE by 2+Full message. Sender ID'd. No trace left.
NR beats ICE by 1Full message. Partial trace — recipient may notice.
TieMetadata only: who, when, message length. No content.
ICE beats NR by 1Nothing. Intercept fails silently.
ICE beats NR by 2+Nothing. If ICE-5 active: interceptor's NR takes a wound.

How It Works In Play

You compose your message and declare an ICE rating. Your NR is committed to Protocol for the round — they cannot run any Engage operation. The GM delivers your message to the recipient in their Private Intel Report.

An intercepting player uses their Engage slot with their Netrunner in Ghost/Approach targeting your channel. The GM compares NR rating vs ICE rating and resolves silently.

The GM never tells a sender their message was intercepted — unless the intercept left a trace. You find out because someone knew something they shouldn't have known.

Section VI // Outcome Engine

How the City Decides

No dice. The GM calculates column scores from your assigned asset ratings, applies modifiers, resolves the matchup against the opposition grid, and returns a Rating. The Rating determines narrative outcome, crew status, and phase cascade into the next phase.

Net Score Rating Phase Outcome Crew Status Narrative Signal
+3 or more SUPERIOR Full objective + bonus intel or asset gained All crew safe; one may advance rating The city didn't notice. It went too smooth.
+2 EXCELLENT Full objective achieved, clean exit All crew safe Clean job. Nobody's looking.
+1 GOOD Objective achieved with minor complication All crew safe; minor gear loss Word is spreading. Someone's asking questions.
0 ADEQUATE Partial success or Pyrrhic victory One crew member stressed or injured Both sides paid. Neither won.
–1 POOR Objective failed; partial intel preserved One crew member captured or wounded They know your face. Not your name. Yet.
–2 NONE Full failure; consequences activated next round One crew member lost or turned Someone burned you. Figure out who.
–3 or less FLATLINED Catastrophic. Cascading consequences. Multiple losses. Heat +2. Night City just wrote you a message in blood.

Phase Cascade

  • SUPERIOR / EXCELLENT — +1 to all column scores in next phase
  • GOOD — No modifier carries forward
  • ADEQUATE — –1 to all column scores in next phase
  • POOR — –1 and GM activates one contingency trigger
  • NONE / FLATLINED — Next phase auto-resolves ADEQUATE against you regardless of grid

Score Modifiers

  • +1 — Asset in its Phase Affinity (right tool, right moment)
  • +1 — Asset rated 4+ in its dominant column
  • +1 — Prior round CT intel against this specific target
  • –1 — Per current Heat level (the city is watching)
  • –1 — Asset in wrong column for its type (noise, friction)
  • –1 — Per wound carried by assigned crew member
